# Anti-Patterns Catalog
# OGZ Saudi Instagram — What Fails and Why
# Evidence from 636-observation corpus | Updated: 2026-05-25

---

## What This Is

Every pattern here is confirmed by data — not opinion. Each entry shows the engagement rate from actual Saudi Instagram corpus observations and explains the underlying failure mechanism.

Use this before briefing production. If the proposed concept matches any entry below, stop and redesign.

---

## Tier 1 — Complete Failures (0–20% high-engagement)

### ❌ discount_price_dominant
**Engagement rate: 0% high-engagement** (n=18, worst pattern in corpus)
**What it is:** Lead messaging is a price or discount. "50% off." "SR 15 only." Any post where the number is the headline.
**Why it fails:**
- Saudi audience interprets price-led content as desperation signaling
- Destroys brand equity — positions brand as commodity
- High-performing Saudi brands almost never lead with price
- Even value brands (McDonalds KSA, AlBaik) use product, occasion, or cultural angles — not price
**The fix:** Lead with the product story, the occasion, or the hospitality cue. Bury the price in the caption, not the visual.

---

### ❌ csr_brand_story
**Engagement rate: 20%** (n=5, −42pp vs baseline)
**What it is:** Content about the brand doing good — charity events, sustainability posts, community work.
**Why it fails:**
- Saudi audience does not engage with corporate virtue-signaling
- These posts feel disconnected from the brand's actual identity
- No hospitality cue, no occasion hook, no product — just brand talking about itself
**The fix:** Embed brand values into product content rather than standalone CSR posts. Show heritage, craft, and people — not press releases.

---

### ❌ campaign_teaser
**Engagement rate: 20%** (n=5, −42pp vs baseline)
**What it is:** "Something big is coming…" posts. Cryptic countdown content. Vague build-up.
**Why it fails:**
- Saudi Instagram audience does not respond to withholding information
- Teaser posts have no product, no hospitality, no occasion value
- By the time the reveal lands, momentum is gone
**The fix:** Launch with substance on Day 1. If you have something worth teasing, open with it directly.

---

### ❌ lifestyle_embed
**Engagement rate: 11%** (n=9, −38pp vs baseline)
**What it is:** Product placed incidentally in a lifestyle scene — sitting on a shelf, visible in background, part of a larger scene but not the hero.
**Why it fails:**
- Product loses visual authority when embedded rather than featured
- Saudi audience expects the brand to own the frame — not borrow someone else's lifestyle
- Often creatively lazy — styled shoot with product as afterthought
**The fix:** Make the product the clear visual hero OR make the people/occasion the clear hero. Never split the difference.

---

### ❌ gamified_content
**Engagement rate: 20%** (n=10, −32pp vs baseline)
**What it is:** "Tag a friend who…" "Comment your answer…" "Spot the difference…" engagement bait.
**Why it fails:**
- Generates low-quality comment engagement (not meaningful reach)
- Saudi audience does not participate in these formats at meaningful rates
- Content feels cheap vs. high-production peers
**The fix:** Build content that earns organic conversation — heritage hooks, occasion relevance, hospitality moments that people genuinely share.

---

### ❌ price_offer_graphic
**Engagement rate: 29%** (n=7, −29pp vs baseline)
**What it is:** Graphic-heavy posts where the design IS the offer — promotional flyer aesthetic.
**Why it fails:**
- Looks like an ad, performs like an ad (people scroll past)
- No story, no humanity, no hospitality cue
- Associated with fast-food value chains — damages premium positioning
**The fix:** Wrap the offer inside a genuine content moment (occasion, limited-time launch) and let the product visual lead.

---

### ❌ warm_najdi_invitational
**Engagement rate: 25%** (n=8, −28pp vs baseline)
**What it is:** Posts that invite but don't deliver — "Come join us," "Visit us," "We're waiting for you" with no product or occasion hook.
**Why it fails:**
- Invitation without a compelling reason registers as generic
- Common pattern that every account uses — zero differentiation
- No visual payoff to justify the CTA
**The fix:** Show what they're being invited to — the food, the atmosphere, the occasion. Don't ask people to come; show them why they'd want to.

## Tier 2 — Underperformers (21–40%)

### ⚠️ educational_explainer
**Engagement rate: 19%** (n=26, −28pp vs baseline)
**What it is:** Carousel or video that teaches the audience something. "Did you know…" "How we make…" explainer-style content.
**Why it fails:**
- Saudi food/beauty/retail audiences are not looking to be educated by brands
- Educational framing signals utility over hospitality and experience
- Often breaks the visual aesthetic with text-heavy or diagram-style design
**The fix:** Embed knowledge inside experience. Show the craft without explaining it. "How it's made" videos work best as *atmosphere* not tutorial.

---

### ⚠️ collab_campaign_reveal
**Engagement rate: 20%** (n=5, −32pp vs baseline)
**What it is:** Announcement posts for a brand collaboration. "We're excited to announce our partnership with…"
**Why it fails:**
- Announcement format is corporate, not experiential
- Audience cares about the collab output, not the press release
- Often front-loads logos and brand names before showing any compelling content
**The fix:** Skip the announcement. Launch the collab with its best creative output immediately.

## Format Failures

### ❌ Long-form video as default
**Video engagement rate: 38%** vs carousel 74% (−36pp gap)
**The mistake:** Defaulting to video because "video gets reach."
**The reality:** In this corpus, video is the worst-performing format. Carousels outperform video by 36pp.
**When video DOES work:** Reels (65%), not long-form video (38%). Reels = short, punchy, soundtrack-driven. Long video = weak.

### ❌ Tuesday publishing
**Tuesday engagement rate: 38%** vs Sunday 61% (−23pp gap)
**The mistake:** Publishing by production calendar rather than audience behavior calendar.
**The fix:** Schedule hero content for Sunday or Friday. Monday–Tuesday is flush-out territory for lower-priority content.

## Cultural / Compliance Failures

### ❌ Left-hand serving
**Hard block — severe**
Any image showing a left hand used for serving food or drink. This is a hard cultural violation with no acceptable exception.
**Common mistake:** Food photography with natural hand placement; videographers not specifying right-hand-only to talent.

### ❌ Cross-gender non-mahram contact
**Hard block — severe**
Physical contact between non-related men and women in the frame. This includes: touching, close physical proximity suggesting contact, hand-holding.
**Common mistake:** International lifestyle shoot templates applied without cultural review.

### ❌ Alcohol product in frame
**Hard block — severe**
Any bottle, glass, or vessel associated with alcohol visible in frame — including sparkling water brands (Perrier, San Pellegrino) that use green bottles and are visually associated with alcohol.
**Common mistake:** International food styling with European water brands. Always use clear-glass Arabic water brands.

### ❌ Generic Eid content for Eid Al-Adha
**Engagement killer**
Treating Eid Al-Adha the same as Eid Al-Fitr. Corpus shows 11% vs 78% engagement — these are completely different occasions.
Eid Al-Adha is about sacrifice, faith, and family in a very specific religious context. Commercial content does not land.

## The 5 Questions to Ask Before Briefing Production

1. **Is the price visible in the visual?** If yes — remove it or redesign around the product.
2. **Is this a teaser, announcement, or invitation with no payoff?** If yes — add substance or cancel the post.
3. **Is the product the clear hero OR is a person/occasion the clear hero?** If neither — redesign composition.
4. **Is this video or long-form?** If yes — justify it vs. carousel. Default to carousel unless motion is essential.
5. **Is the publish day Tuesday?** If yes — move to Sunday or Friday.
