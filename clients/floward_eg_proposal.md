# الأوردر باسمك يا ريّس
## *This Time, the Order Is Yours*
### Floward Egypt — Father's Day Film, June 21, 2026 | OGZ Studios

---

## 1. THE CONCEPT

**The men who deliver every "I love you" in Egypt have never received one.**

Floward's drivers, florists and hub workers hand-deliver thousands of gifts a year. They watch every father's face at every door. Most of them are fathers themselves — Egyptian fathers, the one archetype who provides by disappearing, deflects with a joke, and answers every offer with **«أنا مش محتاج حاجة»**. Inside Floward's own hubs sits the company's only undelivered order.

So we don't make them a tribute film. They'd deflect it on camera and the film dies. Instead:

**THE MECHANIC — three moves:**

1. **THE COVER STORY.** We invite them — officially, paid, on the clock, confirmed by their managers — as Floward's **لجنة الخبراء**: the Expert Committee. Brief: design «أحسن هدية عيد أب في مصر» for the 2026 collection, before marketing does. Name cards. Lanyards: **«خبير هدايا»**. Tea served TO them. And a documentary crew films the committee **openly** — «إحنا بنصوّر اللجنة عشان إطلاق المجموعة». Cameras on tripods, lavaliers on every collar, a sound recordist visibly at work. The fathers know they are being filmed from minute one. What they don't know is what the last order of the day holds. They walk in as the authority, never the subject.

2. **THE DIRECTED BRAINSTORM.** Every question is framed as market research about "العميل" — the client. But the client they describe is themselves. Question by question, Egypt's most experienced gift experts unknowingly write their own delivery order: the gift, the card, the words they never heard. Their expertise becomes involuntary autobiography. The cameras — in the room all day, with everyone's knowledge — are already rolling when «هو» becomes «أنا».

3. **THE REVEAL.** While they brainstorm, the gift each father described is assembled one room away by Floward's own florists. At the end of the shift, one last order arrives — processed like any order. The name on the slip is his. The gift inside is the one he designed an hour ago. The card is in his child's handwriting. And then the children walk in.

**One day. One hub. Real uniforms, real order slips, real fathers, open cameras. The set is the brand. The premise is the story press retells for free — and the only public framing this film will ever have:**

**«سألنا خبراءنا يصمّموا أحسن هدية لعيد الأب… ومعرفوش إنها ليهم.»**

This is not an ad about Floward. It is a real Floward order — designed, curated, assembled and delivered on camera. The service promise, performed instead of claimed.

**Closing super:** «بيوصّلوا الحب لبيوت مصر كلها… النهارده الأوردر باسمهم. كل سنة وانت طيب يا ريّس — فلاورد»

## 2. WHY IT WORKS

- **It IS the brief, upgraded.** The CSR ask — "celebrate our ops heroes, their effort and talent" — is delivered with the fathers as paid consultants, credited experts and designers. Employer brand and brand film in one shoot day. Zero charity framing, zero poverty porn.
- **The gift is real — and it is not just flowers.** Each father receives a bonus, a paid family day, and his name printed in the app on **«مجموعة الورديّة»** — the collection the committee designed — with a per-unit donation made in the fathers' names for every set sold. This is announced inside the story, not buried in a press note. The film's answer to «ادّيله علاوة مش وردة» is in the film itself.
- **CSR becomes category ownership.** Father's Day is the quietest occasion in the gifting calendar — Floward's own order data will confirm and quantify this before any number reaches a screen — and the reason is the code this film names: fathers give silently and receive nothing. Every viewer leaves implicated — «امتى آخر مرة بعتّ حاجة لأبوك؟» — and Floward is the only adequate answer.
- **Built to travel.** Nothing in it is acted: real men, real reveal, real tears dressed as jokes. The one-line premise is shareable on its own, the reveal clips are vertical-native, and the brainstorm format invites UGC — "ask your father the expert questions" — with zero celebrity fee.
- **The dignity engine.** Fathers-as-experts is the structural insurance: status is raised at every beat, never stripped. They enter as a committee, exit as superstars — «يا ريّس» shouted across the hub. Emotion comes from their own words, spoken on camera with their knowledge, never scripted. No competitor can copy this without Floward's hubs, uniforms and order slips.
- **June 21 conversion is built in.** The film proves on real faces that «مش محتاج حاجة» is a lie. He won't ask — so you send. **«هو مش هيطلب. انت اللي هتبعت.»** The gift on screen is built from Floward's standing collection, orderable today; «مجموعة الورديّة» follows on the date merchandising confirms.

## 3. THE RECOMMENDATION — ONE ROUTE

We developed five executions on this spine. We recommend one.

### Route 1 — The Undelivered Order | «الأوردر اللي ما وصلش»

**Logline:** The fathers who deliver Egypt's feelings design the perfect Father's Day gift — not knowing the order slip already carries their own names.

**Three directed questions:**
- «إنتو وصّلتوا آلاف الهدايا بإيديكم. افتكروا آخر مرة شفتوا أب هو اللي بيستلم الهدية مش بيبعتها… كان شكله إيه؟»
- «الأب المصري عمره ما بيقول بحبك بكلامها… طب بيقولها إزاي؟ هاتولنا أمثلة من اللي بتشوفوه.»
- «اكتبولنا الكارت اللي هييجي مع الهدية دي. لو الأب ده هيوصله سطر واحد بس من ولاده… يكون إيه؟»

**The twist:** The facilitator reads the delivery slip out loud, work-procedure flat — «أوردر باسم… عم حسن» — and the card inside is the father's own sentence, in his child's handwriting, signed by his children's names. His words come back to him in his children's voices.

**Why this one, stated plainly:**
1. **Purest insight.** Every beat — the slip, the card, the sentence returned — is the brief's truth performed, with nothing decorative between the viewer and the idea.
2. **Maximum brand-lock.** The order slip, the box, the chain of custody, the hub itself: every emotional object in the film is a Floward object. No competitor can re-shoot this; it has Floward's name printed on its load-bearing prop.
3. **Festival-grade restraint.** The twist is procedural, not theatrical — which is exactly why it lands, and exactly why it travels.

**The alternate:** **Route 4 — «صباح الورد — ورديّة بابا»** — full treatment in the Appendix. Choose it if the client wants owned language («صباح الورد» as a brand asset) plus the named collection at the film's center.

**Also developed** (full treatments available on request):
- **Route 2 — «من غير كارت»:** the men who carry other people's love letters receive the first delivery of their lives that arrives WITH a card — a multi-year platform play.
- **Route 3 — «لما الهدية ترجع لصاحبها»:** documentary truth above all — the expert's card laid beside the child's card, nearly the same sentence, one insert shot.
- **Route 5 — «راجعة لصحابها»:** the leanest viral build — «اقلبوا الكارت», each card carrying the father's own morning sentence, verbatim.

## 4. THE EMOTIONAL ENGINEERING

The brainstorm is the script-mining machine. One ladder, four rungs — the director never announces the climb, and the room is never pushed an inch past where it wants to go.

**RUNG 1 — WARM-UP (pride, status, work):**
«إنتو وصّلتوا آلاف الهدايا… ليه في رأيكو الأب عمره ما بييجي له هدايا؟» — they answer as professionals, confident, joking. We bank the jokes; they become the film's opening.

**RUNG 2 — EXPERTISE (the client persona):**
«احكيلي عن العميل بتاعنا — بيشتغل إيه؟ بيرجع الساعة كام؟» · «إيه الحاجة اللي الأب ده نفسه فيها وعمره ما هيطلبها بلسانه؟» — they build "the client" in third person. The client is a working father who comes home tired. The room doesn't notice it's drawing a self-portrait.

**RUNG 3 — THE MIRROR (the questions come home):**
«آخر مرة حد جابلك انت هدية كانت إمتى؟» — a room full of gift experts, and nobody has an answer. Give the silence room; it is saying what he can't. · «افتكروا أبوكم — كان بيرجع شايل إيه في إيده؟» — the لفّة mirror: he realizes out loud he does exactly what his father did. · «الأب المصري بيقول بحبك إزاي من غير ما ينطقها؟»

**RUNG 4 — THE TURN (he writes the card):**
«اكتبولنا الكارت — لو الأب ده هيوصله سطر واحد بس من ولاده، يكون إيه؟» — five men who don't write much, holding pens. The first total silence of the day. Whatever he writes is the card that comes back to him, signed by his children.

**THE BEATS THE DIRECTOR WAITS FOR (cameras pre-positioned, never chasing):**
- **The pronoun slip** — mid-sentence, «العميل بيرجع تعبان» becomes «أنا برجع ألاقي الواد صاحي مستنيني». The exact second «هو» becomes «أنا» is the film's thesis. Protect it.
- **The joke that arrives one beat too late** — the laugh that covers the swallow. The tell is the delay. Stay with him after the punchline; the delay is the honesty.
- **The hands** — rough fingers doing tender things: stripping thorns, folding ribbon, a pen hesitating over a card. Macro lens, all day.
- **What gets crossed out** before what gets written.
- **The voice that changes on one word** while dictating "the client's" card.
- **The deflection at the peak** — «في حاجة وقعت في عيني»، «هو في حد بيعيّط من الورد؟». The joke that hides the tear IS the Egyptian "I love you". Never cut it out; it is the truest frame in the film.

**THE FLOOR THE FILM STANDS ON.** No beat above depends on tears. If no father breaks — and a room of Egyptian fathers may well not — the film stands on the cards, the silence and the pronoun slip alone. That is editorial law, written into the treatment: tears are a gift the film may receive, never a quota it goes hunting for.

## 5. THE REVEAL — MASTER VERSION, BEAT BY BEAT

**BEAT 1 — THE FALSE ENDING.** The facilitator caps the marker: «تمام يا جماعة، الفريق هياخد التصميم بتاعكم وينفّذه. بس قبل ما نمشي — في باتش أوردرات أخيرة نخلصها.» The experts relax. The crew keeps documenting "the committee wrap" — same cameras, same positions, nothing signals a "moment." (Throughout the session, behind a partition, Floward's own florists have been assembling each father's described gift AS he described it — his words becoming a physical object one room away.)

**BEAT 2 — THE LAST ORDER.** Junior colleagues — the drivers and florists these men trained, in full Floward uniform — wheel in a trolley of Floward boxes. The fathers laugh: work followed them into the meeting. Dignity is in the chain of custody: a peer hand-off, never a producer.

**BEAT 3 — THE NAME.** Routine procedure: scan, verify, dispatch. The beep. The facilitator reads the slip work-flat: «أوردر باسم… عم حسن.» Confusion. Then the second name. The room goes quiet one father at a time, like lights going off. The confusion is allowed to breathe — no one explains.

**BEAT 4 — THE BOX.** Inside: the exact gift HE designed an hour ago. And the card — his own sentence from the last exercise, in his child's handwriting, signed by his children's names. Insert shot: his "expert" card laid beside the real one. Nearly the same sentence.

**BEAT 5 — THE VOICES.** The brainstorm screen — the same screen that held the "customer persona" — cuts to his children's recorded messages. The first word is always **«بابا»** — the only word in the film that breaks every father the same way. He hears the voice before he sees anything; the head turn is the shot.

**BEAT 6 — THE CHILDREN: ALL OR NONE.** One rule, locked at casting: **every father gets his children in the room, or no father does — and every reveal uses the recorded messages.** No two-tier reveals; a hug for one man and a screen for the next, filmed side by side, is not this film. Family presence is confirmed for every cast father before the shoot is locked, and the recorded-message version is storyboarded as an equal film, not a fallback apology. When the children come: the doors open and the kids walk the conveyor path the orders travel, each carrying a Floward bouquet — the product enters the film in a child's hands, never as set dressing. The expert lanyards are still around their necks.

**BEAT 7 — CAMERA AND SOUND LAW.** The cameras were never hidden — they were in the room all day, documenting the committee. At the reveal the law is stillness: long lenses from the same rigged positions, zero crew movement, no one approaches the fathers for the first sixty seconds. No score until after the first tear-hiding joke lands — the hub's ambient hum and cold-room fans ARE the music; strings enter under, never over. Dignity rule: any father who is fully overcome gets the camera held on his HANDS, not his face.

**BEAT 8 — FINAL WIDE.** The hub floor. The men who deliver Egypt's feelings, holding boxes with their own names on the slip, among ten thousand flowers packed for other people. Colleagues clapping: «يا ريّس!» — status confirmed at the exit, never stripped. Cut to black.

**Super, in silence:** «بيوصّلوا آلاف الهدايا لآباء مصر… وعمرهم ما استلموا ولا واحدة. لحد النهارده.»
**Logo. Conversion line:** «كل أب بيقول «أنا مش محتاج حاجة». هو مش هيطلب. انت اللي هتبعت. — عيد الأب ٢١ يونيو»

## 6. PRODUCTION — THE HONEST MATH

**LOCATION — ops keeps its floor.** Two viable options: **(A) recommended — a training or dark hub dressed live** (full set control, pre-lit from call time, zero disruption to fulfillment), or **(B) a live hub shot BEFORE peak week.** We do not put a crew inside a live hub during Father's Day fulfillment week; ops owns the floor and the film respects that.

**STAGING — one geography, reconciled.** Two spaces, both rigged from call time. The brainstorm room holds the signature locked-frame before/after: Beats 1–5 (false ending, trolley, name, box, voices) all play inside that same locked master. Beats 6–8 (the children's entrance on the conveyor path, the final wide) move to the hub floor, covered by a second pre-rigged, pre-lit unit. The locked-frame promise and the reveal staging no longer compete — they live in different rooms by design.

**CAMERAS — OPEN, NOT HIDDEN.** The cover story does the work: the committee is being documented for the launch. Tripods in plain sight, lavaliers on every collar, a sound recordist visibly working — and within the hour the room forgets them, as every documentary room does. The fathers know they are filmed; they don't know what the last order holds. Open filming buys us, at zero cost to the surprise: clean, subtitle-able dialogue audio (this film IS its dialogue), no covert-recording exposure, signed releases for every person on the floor, and **singles-first coverage so each father is editorially isolatable**. Coverage: locked wide master (identical framing, brainstorm and reveal) + two long-lens singles on faces + one dedicated insert camera on hands.

**CAST — built for failure paths.** **8 Floward Egypt fathers cast to use 4–6** (drivers, florists, hub workers). The family pre-screen is run by production through a consent-respecting conversation with each candidate — what we ask, we ask him; we never mine HR files for private family situations. Paid as consultants, on the clock. Facilitator briefed on the question ladder; supervisor and junior colleagues in on it.

**THE GIFTS — modular by design.** Three pre-designed gift bases, built from Floward's standing collection, cover anything the committee describes; the florists behind the partition personalize a base, they never improvise from zero. If a father describes the unbuildable — «ابني يرجع من السعودية» — the card carries his sentence and the base carries the gesture. The mechanic cannot break on camera.

**THE REAL GIFT — announced, not implied.** Beyond the box: a **bonus** for each committee member, a **paid family day**, and **«مجموعة الورديّة»** carrying the fathers' names in the app, with a **per-unit donation in their names** for every set sold. This is part of the public story from day one.

**CONSENT — TWO GATES. THE DIGNITY LAW.**
- **Gate 1, at casting:** a true general filming consent — «فيلم عن ناسنا، للنشر العام» — signed before the shoot, with no reveal details.
- **Gate 2, after the reveal:** the specific release, with a **48-hour cool-off period**, signed **away from managers**, accompanied by a **written non-retaliation guarantee from HR** and an **unconditional kill-fee** — paid in full whether he signs or not. Any father who declines: every frame of him is pulled; the singles-first coverage makes that a re-cut, not a re-shoot.
- **Children:** guardians sign at the family pre-shoot. Because the father — the children's legal guardian — is the uninformed party, a **written legal opinion on the guardian-consent structure is a pre-production gate**, not a detail.
- **Quote protocol:** every verbatim line that travels from the room into the film or its copy passes a **pre-agreed quote-approval protocol with the client** — and each father approves his own quotes at Gate 2.

A film about dignity cannot be made without this. It is non-negotiable, and it is also the story.

**DELIVERABLES.** 90s hero film · 3 × 30s cutdowns (the silence, the slip, the children) · 9:16 vertical reveal edit for Reels/TikTok · stills package. The public framing is one sentence and only one sentence: «سألنا خبراءنا يصمّموا أحسن هدية لعيد الأب… ومعرفوش إنها ليهم.» «مجموعة الورديّة» is announced in the film and goes live on the date Floward merchandising confirms in writing — we make no June 21 SKU promise on their behalf.

**TIMELINE — honest, gates named.** Air date June 21 holds only if approval lands within **48 hours of this deck**:
- **June 11 (today):** deck lands. **Floward Group (Kuwait HQ) legal & PR review starts in parallel with Egypt marketing — this is an explicit gate, opened now, not after Egypt says yes.**
- **June 13:** approval gate closes — client + Group sign-off.
- **June 13–15:** casting (8 fathers), Gate-1 consents, legal opinion on guardian consent, family recruitment.
- **June 15–16:** family pre-shoots (children's messages + handwritten cards) under the same cover story; guardian releases signed.
- **June 17–18:** main shoot day + contingency half-day.
- **June 18–20:** edit, grade, subtitle; client and quote approvals.
- **June 21:** **hero film airs.** Cutdowns, vertical and stills follow June 23–26.
- If approval slips past June 13, June 21 keeps a shorter hero cut or the film moves to a strength date — we will say so then, not discover it on June 19. The dignity laws are never what gets cut.
